ArcGIS Online Social Activity Stream

To ensure the success of the Social Activity Stream, I have worked as part of the Holistic Design team to clarify user needs, define a minimum viable product, and design an elegant user interface.

The Goal

Conceptualize and design a social activity feed for Portal and ArcGIS Online which will enable our users to stay informed, connected, and productive.

Status: March 2016 Release Candidate

Skills Used

  • UI Design
  • Feature Ideation
  • Comparative Analysis
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration
  • Rapid Prototyping

Requirements Document

As part of the Holistic Design team, I participated in a two-day workshop during which I collaborated with cross-functional team members to review and prioritize user needs, brainstorm potential features, and define the minimal viable product.

It was also our goal to begin writing the extensive requirements document. This document has been instrumental in collaborating with team members to establish feasibility and determine areas of platform development.

Wireframe to Design Prototype

During the workshop, I took the initiative to create a prototype wireframe which captured the spirit of the minimal viable product. Since that time, I have collaborated with the UX architect to refine the prototype's design, used the prototype to socialize the design to cross-functional stakeholders, and rapidly iterate through designs based on aesthetic and technical feedback.

Designing the prototype to consume actual data has allowed me to decouple the content from the design. This has facilitated testing a range of use cases and has accelerated the design, review, and iteration process.
